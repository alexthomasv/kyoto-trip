⛩️ 1:50 – 2:35 PM · Fushimi Inari — Senbon Torii Gates
Fushimi Inari — Senbon Torii Tunnel伏見稲荷大社 千本鳥居 · Free · 45 min
THE most iconic image of Japan — thousands of vermillion torii gates tunneling up a mountainside. Focused visit: walk the main Senbon Torii tunnel (the iconic double-row gates), snap photos in the vermillion corridor, and head back. 45 min is perfect for the gates experience without the full mountain hike.
📸 Photo tip: The best shots are in the tunnel itself. Wait for a brief gap in foot traffic — afternoon light filtering through the orange gates is gorgeous.

🏯 2:45 – 4:15 PM · Kiyomizu-dera + Higashiyama Lanes
Kiyomizu-dera — The Stage Temple清水寺 · ¥400 entry · Closes 6pm
A massive wooden terrace jutting out over a hillside with panoramic views of Kyoto. The main hall was built without a single nail. In late March, cherry trees around the terrace bloom pink against the city skyline. After the temple, walk downhill through Ninen-zaka and Sannen-zaka — Kyoto's most photogenic stone-paved lanes lined with traditional shops.

Shichimiya Honpo — Shichimi Since 1655七味家本舗 · Top of Kiyomizu-zaka · 370 years old
Kyoto-style shichimi togarashi emphasizes sansho pepper and nori over raw heat — a fundamentally different blend from Tokyo's Yagenbori. Operating at this exact spot since 1655. Perfect edible souvenir you literally cannot get outside this shop.
¥500–700Top of Kiyomizu-zaka🌶️ GET: Kyoto Shichimi tin (京七味)
Kanshundo — Seasonal Namagashi Art甘春堂 · est. 1865 · Higashiyama · Walk-in
March-specific namagashi (fresh wagashi): sakura and spring flower designs that are edible art. Kyoto is the undisputed capital of wagashi — these aesthetics are not replicated elsewhere. Sit in-shop with matcha for ¥800.

Kagizen Yoshifusa — Gion's Oldest Sweet Shop
鍵善良房 · 祇園四条 · Tabelog 3.65
Kyoto's most revered wagashi shop. Their kuzukiri (くずきり) — translucent kuzu jelly strips in cold syrup, either black sugar or white — is one of Kyoto's most distinctive sweets. Sit in the back tatami room for ¥1,200. You won't find this in Osaka.

Nanaya Kyoto — World's Strongest Matcha Gelatoななや京都 · 7 levels of matcha · Only in Kyoto + Shizuoka
Seven intensity levels of matcha gelato — from level 1 (mild) to level 7 (the most concentrated matcha ice cream on the planet). Level 7 is pitch dark green, intensely bitter, and unforgettable. On Ninen-zaka, right on your walking route down from Kiyomizu.
¥400–600On Ninen-zaka (your walk)🍦 GET: Level 7 Premium (No. 7 抹茶)
Gion Tsujiri — Matcha Parfait祇園辻利 · est. 1860 · Gion corner · Walk-in
The matcha institution of Gion — est. 1860 as a tea merchant, now Kyoto's most famous matcha café. Their towering matcha parfait with jelly, shiratama, red bean, and matcha ice cream is THE Kyoto matcha experience. Right on Shijo-dori at the Gion corner.
¥1,200–1,500Walk-in (15 min queue)🍨 GET: Tsujiri Matcha Parfait (特選抹茶パフェ)
Issen Yoshoku — Kyoto Street Food Original壹銭洋食 · Gion · Taisho-era savory crepe · Walk-in
A thin savory crepe stuffed with green onion, beef tendon, konjac, shrimp, and egg — invented in Taisho-era Kyoto. Wild retro interior with hundreds of vintage posters. The original shop is right here in Gion on your walk. Fun, fast, cheap, and uniquely Kyoto.
¥700Walk-in · Gion🥞 GET: Issen Yoshoku (壹銭洋食)
🌅 4:30 – 5:30 PM · Gion → Kamo River Sunset
Gion District + Kamo River Sunset祇園 → 鴨川 · Free · Walk from Kiyomizu
Walk through Gion's Hanamikoji-dori — Kyoto's geisha quarter with traditional wooden machiya houses, lanterns, and (if you're lucky) a glimpse of a geiko heading to an evening appointment. Continue west to the Kamo River between Sanjo and Shijo bridges for the sunset. Locals sit on the banks in evenly-spaced pairs — it's a Kyoto tradition.
