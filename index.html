Get Tickets While You're Here
京都駅 · Do this now to save time later
You're at Kyoto Station — sort out all your train tickets now while you're right next to the machines.
🎫 Step-by-step
1. IC card (ICOCA) — buy or top up at any JR green ticket machine in the central concourse (English available, press flag icon). You need this for JR trains today: Arashiyama ¥240, Uji ¥250 each way. Tap to enter, tap to exit — that's it
2. Kintetsu to Osaka — buy the express surcharge ticket — from JR central exit, walk southwest past Isetan dept store toward the Kintetsu gate on B1 (~3 min). Look for the Limited Express ticket machines (特急券発売機) to the RIGHT of the Kintetsu turnstiles. Press English → Osaka-Namba → pick ~2:10 PM → pick seat → pay (cash or IC card). You get a paper ticket — keep it safe
What you'll have: IC card (for the base fare, tap at turnstile) + paper Limited Express ticket (reserved seat, show to conductor). Both needed at the exit gate in Namba — tap IC card AND insert paper ticket into the slot at the same time
🚃
Kintetsu to Osaka — How It Works
近鉄特急 · Two-ticket system explained
Kintetsu uses a two-ticket system. Don't worry — it's simpler than it sounds.
🎫 The two tickets
Ticket 1 — Base fare (乗車券) — ¥760. Your IC card handles this automatically. Just tap at the turnstile to enter and exit
Ticket 2 — Limited Express surcharge (特急券) — ¥520. This is the reserved seat paper ticket you bought at the machine. All seats are reserved, no standing
Total: ~¥1,280 for a comfy reserved seat. Transfer at Yamato-Saidaiji (大和西大寺) to a second Limited Express to Namba — your ticket covers both legs
🚉 At the gates
Entering at Kyoto: Tap IC card on the reader at the Kintetsu turnstile. Walk through. Your paper express ticket is NOT needed here
Exiting at Osaka-Namba: Insert the paper express ticket into the gate slot AND tap your IC card on the reader — do both at the same time. Gate opens, both are processed
¥1,280 totalAll seats reserved~55 min with transfer
💡 Simpler alternative: Skip the Limited Express entirely. Take a Kintetsu Rapid Express (急行) — just tap your IC card, no special ticket needed, no reservation. Runs every 10–15 min. Takes ~1h 10min to Namba with one transfer. Same platform, same gate. Only difference: no reserved seat, slightly slower. ¥760 total.
