⏰ 6:30 – 8:00 AM · Cherry Blossoms at Dawn
Philosopher's Path — Cherry Blossom Tunnel哲学の道 · Free · Best before 8am
2km canal path lined with hundreds of cherry trees. Late March is peak bloom — the canopy forms a pink tunnel over the water. At 6:30am you'll share it with a dozen joggers. By 10am it's shoulder-to-shoulder.
🌸 Cherry blossom tip: Best light is 7–7:30am golden hour. The reflection of pink petals in the canal is the shot.

Nishiki Market錦市場 · 400 years old · 100+ stalls

⛩️ 1:30 – 3:15 PM · Fushimi Inari Taisha
Fushimi Inari Taisha — Thousand Torii Gates伏見稲荷大社 千本鳥居 · Free · 24 hours
THE most iconic image of Japan — thousands of vermillion torii gates tunneling up a mountainside. Free to enter, open 24 hours. The main torii tunnel (Senbon Torii) takes 15–20 min. Going halfway up (to the Yotsutsuji intersection viewpoint) takes about 45 min and gives you panoramic views over all of Kyoto. Afternoon light filtering through the orange gates is gorgeous.
💡 Pro tip: Most tourists only do the first 10 min. Keep going past the first fork — the crowds thin dramatically. The Yotsutsuji viewpoint (halfway) is the sweet spot.
🍴 Approach street food + mid-mountain teahouse
Inari sushi — sweet fried tofu pockets with sushi rice. Named after this shrine. · ¥200–300
Suzume-yaki — sparrow/fox-shaped cakes with sweet red bean · ¥200

Nishimura-ya Teahouse (西村屋) — right at Yotsutsuji viewpoint, halfway up. Cold matcha lemonade + azuki parfait while overlooking all of Kyoto · ¥400–700

🏯 3:30 – 5:00 PM · Kiyomizu-dera + Higashiyama Lanes
Kiyomizu-dera — The Stage Temple清水寺 · ¥400 entry · Closes 6pm
A massive wooden terrace jutting out over a hillside with panoramic views of Kyoto. The main hall was built without a single nail. In late March, cherry trees around the terrace bloom pink against the city skyline. After the temple, walk downhill through Ninen-zaka and Sannen-zaka — Kyoto's most photogenic stone-paved lanes lined with traditional shops.

Kasagiya — Warabi Mochi on the Lanes
かさぎ屋 · 三年坂 · Tabelog 3.6
A tiny wooden shop on Sannen-zaka, open since 1914. Famous for warabi mochi (bracken fern jelly dusted in kinako flour) and oshiruko (sweet red bean soup). Grab a takeaway cup and eat on the stone steps — the most Kyoto moment possible.

Kagizen Yoshifusa — Gion's Oldest Sweet Shop
鍵善良房 · 祇園四条 · Tabelog 3.65
Kyoto's most revered wagashi shop. Their kuzukiri (くずきり) — translucent kuzu jelly strips in cold syrup, either black sugar or white — is one of Kyoto's most distinctive sweets. Sit in the back tatami room for ¥1,200. You won't find this in Osaka.
¥1,000–1,400On the Gion walkWalk-in · closes 6pm
🌅 5:00 – 6:00 PM · Gion → Kamo River Sunset
Gion District + Kamo River Sunset祇園 → 鴨川 · Free · Walk from Kiyomizu
Walk through Gion's Hanamikoji-dori — Kyoto's geisha quarter with traditional wooden machiya houses, lanterns, and (if you're lucky) a glimpse of a geiko heading to an evening appointment. Continue west to the Kamo River between Sanjo and Shijo bridges for the sunset. Locals sit on the banks in evenly-spaced pairs — it's a Kyoto tradition.

📍TOGion Hanamikoji-dori → Kamo River

🎋 7:30 – 9:00 AM · Arashiyama Bamboo Grove
Arashiyama Bamboo Grove嵐山 竹林の小径 · Free · Empty before 9am
Towering bamboo tunnels filtering morning light into green-gold shafts. At 7:30am you'll practically own it. Walk through, cross Togetsukyo Bridge, then stroll the main street as stalls open (~8:30–9am).
